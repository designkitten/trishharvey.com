Montgomery County, Maryland

Montgomery County is Maryland’s most populous jurisdiction, and also its most affluent. The county is bordered by Washington, D.C. and Prince George’s County to the south east; Howard County to the northeast; Frederick County to the northwest; and the Potomac River/Virginia to the southwest. The County has a wide range of land uses.  It has suburban CBD’s in Silver Spring and Bethesda.   It has suburban office markets in the I-270 and Route 29 corridors.  It also has agricultural uses in the northern parts of the County. The I270 corridor accounts for most of the recent growth in the County. As in other areas of the D.C. metropolitan area, technology is the principal economic activity in the corridor.

The Federal Government has major installations such as the Bethesda Naval Hospital, National Institutes of Health, National Institutes of Standards and Technologies, Department of Health and Human Services, Food and Drug Administration, Nuclear Regulatory Commission, and the Department of Energy. Despite the large presence of the Federal Government the County’s private sector is strong. Some of the largest employers in the county are the who’s who of corporate America including: COMSAT, IBM, Marriott, LORAL, Vitro, Hughes Network Systems, General Electric Information Systems, Lockheed Martin, and Bechtel just to name a few.

Montgomery County is Maryland’s most populous jurisdiction with population of over 800,000 residents. Its population growth rate is greater than that of any other jurisdiction in the state. Montgomery County is the gateway to the state of Maryland for migrants from other states or other parts of the world. The county has a highly educated population - 23% have advance degrees(3rd in the nation) and 50% have bachelor’s degree or higher(4th in the nation). The county also has the purchasing power with the 5th highest median income in the nation.

ROCKVILLE, MARYLAND

Rockville is an affluent and very desirable residential community containing a thriving retail corridor. Rockville Pike is the major north/south artery through the area. Often called Montgomery County's "main street", Rockville Pike is a bustling, dynamic corridor with departments stores, specialty shops, restaurants, movie theaters, office supply stores, car dealers, computer stores and office buildings. Merchants who want to locate on "the Pike" rarely find vacant space.

Along the length of the Rockville Pike Corridor there are numerous anchored  shopping centers. Rockville Pike is a retail mecca, which contains one of the highest concentrations of retail space in Montgomery County with approximately 1.3 million square feet of space. At present, Rockville Pike is one of the busiest and most productive retail-commercial corridors in the Metropolitan Washington region. New stores arrive regularly to join the thriving atmosphere.

Rockville is an established residential/suburban area, densely developed with a mixture of single-family and multi-family residential developments. Located only 12 miles northwest of Washington, D.C., it is the second largest city in Maryland and the county seat of Montgomery County.
