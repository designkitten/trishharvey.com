Living With Epielpsy

Information for: Kids | Teenagers | Women | Parents | Seniors

The Epilepsy Foundation is committed to:

  • Reducing the treatment gap between what can be done for people with epilepsy and what gets done;
  • Ensuring access to appropriate care and treatment options;
  • Ensuring that treatment addresses the full spectrum of daily life, not just seizures;
  • Empowering people with epilepsy to be self-advocates for appropriate care, and;
  • Improving how people with epilepsy are perceived, accepted and valued in society.

Wellness & Lifestyle

For people with epilepsy, everyday issues like exercise, dating, employment, insurance and driving include special considerations. Our Wellness & Lifestyle section addresses many important areas of concern.

Career Support Center

When do you talk about epilepsy in the workplace? Should you mention epilepsy in a job interview? Find these answers, plus a complete job seekers workbook and resource guide.

'No Limits' Telementoring

The Foundation recently started a program using technology to linking young adults with epilepsy and adult mentors – to help young people build self-esteem, receive career and life advice, and become more comfortable with living and talking about their condition.
